# BUG-004 — Constraint solver depends on hash-map iteration order

- **Severity:** medium (replay regression; gameplay-affecting)
- **Subsystem:** physics
- **File:** `src/engine_demo/physics/constraint.cpp`
- **Class:** non-determinism
- **Anchor for:** Session 04 (Proactive — logic track)

## Reproduction

Two solvers, identical bodies and constraints inserted in opposite orders, then 8
iterations: positions diverge by O(1e-9) per iteration, accumulating across frames.

## Root cause

`solve()` iterates constraints in vector order — fine — but reads body state via
`m_bodies.find(id)` on an `eastl::hash_map`. Hash-map traversal does not guarantee a fixed
order across runs. The projection thus applies in subtly different sequence depending on
collision distribution.

## Fix

Sort constraints once at insertion by `(min(a, b), max(a, b))`, and read body state
through a deterministic dense index built at solver setup. Article 5 (determinism)
requires no run-to-run divergence under fixed seeds.
